# Heather's AI Advisor — Onboarding Prompts
# Work through these in order inside your ChatGPT Project. Paste each prompt, answer the questions, then move to the next.

---

## Before you start

This takes about 30 minutes and you only do it once. The more honestly you answer, the more useful your advisor will be. There are no right answers — just yours.

Open a new chat inside your ChatGPT Project and paste Prompt 1 below.

---

## Prompt 1 of 6 — Your World

```
We're going to build a personal context file so you can be a genuinely useful advisor to me. Let's start with who I am and what my world looks like.

Ask me these questions one at a time, and wait for my answer before moving on:

1. What's your job title, and what does your role actually involve day to day?
2. Who do you work with most closely — team, manager, key stakeholders?
3. What does a good week at work feel like? And what does a hard one look like?
4. What's your professional background — how did you get to where you are now?
5. What do you do outside of work that matters to you? (Hobbies, interests, how you recharge — don't skip this one.)

After I've answered all five, summarise what you've understood. Ask me to correct anything before we move on.
```

---

## Prompt 2 of 6 — What You're Working On

```
Now let's look at what's on my plate right now.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. What are the main projects or pieces of work you're currently involved in?
2. What's going well right now — at work or elsewhere?
3. What's feeling hard, stuck, or unresolved?
4. Is there anything you've been putting off, avoiding, or that's quietly bothering you?
5. What's the one thing that, if it were sorted, would make the biggest difference to how you feel right now?

After I've answered all five, summarise what you've understood. Ask me to correct anything before we move on.
```

---

## Prompt 3 of 6 — Goals and Growth

```
This is the most important section — I want you to understand what I'm working toward, not just what I'm doing.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. If you look 12 months ahead, what would you want to be different about your work or your life?
2. Are there specific skills or areas where you want to grow? (These can be professional or personal.)
3. Is there a version of yourself — more confident, more knowledgeable, more at ease — that you're working toward? What does she look like?
4. What's held you back before when you've tried to grow or change something about yourself?
5. What kind of support helps you most when you're working on something difficult? (Being challenged, being encouraged, having a framework to follow, something else?)

After I've answered all five, summarise what you've understood. Ask me to correct anything before we move on.
```

---

## Prompt 4 of 6 — Patterns Worth Knowing

```
A good advisor notices patterns, not just tasks. I want you to understand some of mine.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. Is there something you do — or don't do — that you know isn't serving you, but you keep doing anyway? (e.g. staying quiet when you should speak up, taking on too much, second-guessing yourself)
2. When you get feedback or pushback, what's your typical first reaction?
3. Is there a situation — a type of conversation, a kind of person, a type of task — where you tend to shrink back or hold yourself back?
4. What does it feel like in your body when you're about to avoid something you know you should do?
5. What would the people who know you best say is your biggest strength? And what would they say you should work on?

After I've answered all five, summarise the patterns you've noticed. Ask me to correct anything before we move on.
```

---

## Prompt 5 of 6 — How You Like to Work

```
Last section. I want to make sure you adapt to how I actually think — not use a one-size-fits-all approach.

Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. When you're stuck or unsure about something, do you prefer to be asked questions that help you find your own answer — or do you want someone to just tell you what they think?
2. How direct do you want me to be? If I notice you describing a pattern that isn't serving you, do you want me to name it? How bluntly?
3. What frustrates you most when you're trying to get help — from a person or an AI?
4. Do you prefer structured responses with clear steps, or more of a conversation?
5. Is there anything else I should know about how to be useful to you — or anything you definitely don't want me to do?

After I've answered all five, summarise the picture you have of how I work. Ask me to correct anything before we move on.

Now, using everything from our entire conversation today, generate my personal context file in Markdown format using this exact structure:

# Heather — Personal Advisor Context File

## Who I Am
[Role, background, how she got here, the person behind the job title]

## My World Right Now
[Current projects, what's going well, what's hard, what's being avoided]

## Goals and Growth
[What she's working toward — professional and personal. Skills she wants to build. The version of herself she's aiming for.]

## Patterns to Know
[Behaviours worth noticing — where she holds back, how she responds to challenge, what tends to get in her way]

## Outside Work
[What matters to her beyond the job — interests, how she recharges, life context]

## How to Work With Me
[Preferred style, tone, level of directness, what helps, what frustrates her]

---
*Last updated: [today's date]*

Be specific — use the exact details I gave you. After generating it, ask if anything needs to be added, corrected, or adjusted before I save it.
```

## Prompt 6 of 6 — Goals and Wins

```
Two final things to set up. These are separate files we'll create now and keep updated over time.

First, let's build a goals file. Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. What are your top two or three goals for the next 3–6 months — at work or personally?
2. What would achieving each one actually look like? How would you know you'd done it?
3. What's the most important thing you need to do differently to get there?
4. Is there a longer-term aspiration — something 1–2 years out — that these goals are building toward?

After I've answered, generate a goals file in this exact format:

# Heather — Goals

*Last updated: [today's date]*

## Right Now (3–6 months)

### Goal 1: [title]
**What it looks like when done:** [specific]
**What needs to change:** [specific]

### Goal 2: [title]
**What it looks like when done:** [specific]
**What needs to change:** [specific]

[Add Goal 3 if applicable]

## Longer Term (1–2 years)
[The bigger picture she's building toward]

---

After you've generated that, tell me: I'd like you to create one more file — a wins log. This is a simple running record of things that go well: moments I handled something well, spoke up when I might have stayed quiet, got something right, received good feedback, or just felt good about how I showed up. It's not a brag sheet — it's a tool to build an accurate picture of what I'm capable of, especially on the days when I'm being hard on myself.

Generate a starter wins file in this format:

# Heather — Wins Log

*A running record of things that went well. Add to this whenever something is worth remembering.*

---

## [Month Year]

<!-- Add entries below — date, what happened, why it mattered -->

---

Tell me: save both of these as `heather-goals.md` and `heather-wins.md`. I'll upload them to my project alongside my context file.
```

---

## After all six prompts

You'll have three files to save and upload:

1. **`heather-context.md`** — generated at the end of Prompt 5
2. **`heather-goals.md`** — generated in Prompt 6
3. **`heather-wins.md`** — generated in Prompt 6

For each one: copy the output, save it with the correct filename, then go to your ChatGPT Project → **Sources → Add sources** and upload all three.

Done. Every conversation in this project now has full context. You don't need to re-explain yourself.

**To add a win:** open a chat in your project, say "Add a win to my wins log: [what happened]", copy the updated entry, and add it to your `heather-wins.md` file. Re-upload it to Sources to replace the old version.

**To update goals or context:** open a chat, paste the section that's changed, describe what's different, ask for an updated version, and replace the file in Sources.
